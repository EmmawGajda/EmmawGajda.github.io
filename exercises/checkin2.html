Welcome!

It's nice to meet you, I'm Emma! I am a hopeful author and prospective editor.

I am in my second year as an English major at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst, though I received my Associate's at Greenfield Community College. I spend my days writing and reading literature and nonsense, and find great joy in both.

I am currently re-reading Lynne Truss's Eats, Shoots, and Leaves and William Strunk Jr. and E.B. White's The Elements of Style, and, as I am forever attempting to sharpen my writing, I am greatly enjoying them. I find that these two seem to always demand my focus when I'm feeling scttered or unenthused about whatever projects I'm working on. If I could suggest any book to someone interested in the "rules" of writing, it would without a doubt be Strunk's revised The Elements of Style, as I think that his incisive demands and the brilliant E.B. White's somewhat sardonic reflections are exactly what any writer needs to hear.

I hope you give it a try. It is the perfect pocket-book.
